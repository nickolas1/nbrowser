This is a browser-based simulation of the gravitational N-body problem, a topic that has fascinated scientists throughout history and the main focus of my previous career as an astronomer. In this example you can watch as the stars in a small cluster dance around each other as they react to the pull of the other stars' gravity. The 'Kepler planets' option shows four stars, each with three planets orbiting around it.

The stars are advanced in time with a basic leapfrog numerical integration scheme written in javascript, and rendered in the browser using d3.

The rendering is in the style of either the children's book author Oliver Jeffers or that of the graphics officer for the Kepler space telescope.
